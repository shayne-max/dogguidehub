If your dog has a sensitive stomach, choosing the right food can make the difference between constant vet visits and a happy, settled pup. In this guide, we break down what actually causes digestive issues in dogs and which foods consistently perform best.

## What Causes a Sensitive Stomach in Dogs?

Common triggers include low-quality fillers, sudden diet changes, food allergies, and certain protein sources. Dogs with sensitive stomachs often show symptoms like:

- Frequent vomiting or diarrhea
- Excessive gas
- Loss of appetite
- Visible discomfort after eating

## What to Look for in a Sensitive Stomach Formula

1. **Limited ingredients** — fewer ingredients means fewer chances for irritation
2. **Novel proteins** — like duck or salmon instead of chicken or beef
3. **No artificial additives** — avoid dyes, fillers, and preservatives
4. **Added probiotics** — support gut health directly

## When to See a Vet

If symptoms persist for more than a few days even after switching food, it's worth scheduling a vet visit to rule out underlying conditions like food allergies or IBD.
